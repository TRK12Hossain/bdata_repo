## 1. SOURCE TIER SYSTEM

Sources are ranked in three tiers. Always try Tier 1 first. Use Tier 2 to
cross-reference or as fallback. Use Tier 3 only for context, never as the
sole source for a numerical claim.

---

## 2. TIER 1 â€” PRIMARY SOURCES (Highest Trust)

These are official Bangladesh government agencies and their direct publications.
Treat data from these sources as authoritative unless explicitly contradicted
by two or more Tier 2 sources.

### Bangladesh Bureau of Statistics (BBS)
- **Homepage:** http://www.bbs.gov.bd
- **Data Portal:** http://data.bbs.gov.bd
- **What it covers:** Population census, poverty data, household income surveys, national accounts, agricultural data, labor force surveys
- **Key publications:** Statistical Yearbook of Bangladesh, Household Income and Expenditure Survey (HIES), Labour Force Survey (LFS)
- **Known limitations:** Some historical data (pre-2000) has gaps; methodology changed between census years; publication lag can be 12-18 months
- **How to cite:** "Bangladesh Bureau of Statistics (BBS), [Publication Name], [Year]"

### Bangladesh Bank (Central Bank)
- **Homepage:** https://www.bb.org.bd
- **Data portal:** https://www.bb.org.bd/en/index.php/econdata/
- **What it covers:** Inflation (CPI), exchange rates, foreign exchange reserves, monetary policy data, banking sector data, remittance inflows, balance of payments
- **Key publications:** Annual Report, Monetary Policy Statement, Economic Trends (monthly)
- **How to cite:** "Bangladesh Bank, [Publication Name], [Year]"

### Ministry of Finance, Bangladesh
- **Homepage:** https://mof.gov.bd
- **Budget documents:** https://mof.gov.bd/site/page/d4b1f6a3-f9d3-4e9a-b68b-a7bbe1e1b53f
- **What it covers:** Annual national budget, sectoral expenditure breakdown, fiscal deficit, public debt data
- **Key publications:** Bangladesh Economic Review (annual), Budget Speech, Medium-Term Macroeconomic Policy Statement
- **How to cite:** "Ministry of Finance, Bangladesh, Bangladesh Economic Review [Year]"

### Ministry of Primary and Mass Education / Ministry of Education
- **Homepage:** https://mopme.gov.bd / https://moedu.gov.bd
- **What it covers:** Education budget, enrollment statistics, teacher-student ratios, literacy campaigns
- **How to cite:** "Ministry of Education, Bangladesh, [Publication], [Year]"

### Ministry of Health and Family Welfare
- **Homepage:** https://mohfw.gov.bd
- **What it covers:** Health sector budget, vaccination rates, maternal mortality, disease statistics
- **How to cite:** "Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Bangladesh, [Publication], [Year]"

### Bangladesh Economic Zones Authority (BEZA) / BIDA
- **What it covers:** FDI data, special economic zone statistics, investment inflows
- **How to cite:** per publication

## 3. TIER 2 â€” INTERNATIONAL OFFICIAL SOURCES (High Trust, Use for Cross-Reference)

These are major international bodies whose data is widely accepted as credible.
Always use to cross-reference Tier 1 data. If Tier 1 data is unavailable,
Tier 2 may be used as the primary source â€” but must be disclosed in the post.

### World Bank â€” Bangladesh
- **Country page:** https://data.worldbank.org/country/BD
- **Open Data portal:** https://data.worldbank.org (search "Bangladesh")
- **What it covers:** GDP (current USD, constant USD, per capita), poverty rates (using $2.15/day line), human capital index, education spending % of GDP, infrastructure investment
- **Key publications:** Bangladesh Development Update (biannual), World Development Report
- **Strength:** Long time series (1960+), consistent methodology across countries, good for regional comparisons
- **How to cite:** "World Bank Open Data, [Indicator Name], accessed [Year]"

### UNESCO Institute for Statistics (UIS)
- **Data portal:** https://uis.unesco.org
- **Country profile:** https://uis.unesco.org/en/country/bd
- **What it covers:** Education spending (% of GDP, % of government budget), enrollment ratios (primary, secondary, tertiary), literacy rates, teacher training statistics, gender parity in education
- **Note:** UNESCO uses its own methodology which may differ slightly from BBS; World Bank often uses UIS data
- **How to cite:** "UNESCO Institute for Statistics (UIS), [Indicator], [Year]"

### International Monetary Fund (IMF)
- **Bangladesh page:** https://www.imf.org/en/Countries/BGD
- **WEO Database:** https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/WEO
- **What it covers:** GDP growth rate, inflation (WPI, CPI), current account balance, fiscal deficit, government debt as % of GDP, economic forecasts
- **Key publication:** World Economic Outlook (biannual), Article IV Staff Reports for Bangladesh
- **How to cite:** "IMF World Economic Outlook Database, [Year]" or "IMF Article IV Consultation, Bangladesh, [Year]"

### Asian Development Bank (ADB)
- **Bangladesh page:** https://www.adb.org/countries/bangladesh/main
- **Key Data:** https://data.adb.org (search Bangladesh)
- **What it covers:** Infrastructure investment, poverty reduction, agricultural data, regional integration, private sector development
- **Key publication:** Asian Development Outlook, Country Partnership Strategies
- **How to cite:** "Asian Development Bank (ADB), [Publication], [Year]"

### UNDP â€” Human Development Reports
- **Bangladesh HDI:** https://hdr.undp.org/data-center/specific-country-data#/countries/BGD
- **Global HDR:** https://hdr.undp.org
- **What it covers:** Human Development Index (HDI) and its components (education index, life expectancy index, GNI per capita index), Gender Inequality Index, Multidimensional Poverty Index
- **How to cite:** "UNDP Human Development Report [Year], Bangladesh"

### UNICEF Bangladesh
- **Data page:** https://data.unicef.org/country/bgd/
- **What it covers:** Child mortality, malnutrition, child education, WASH (water/sanitation/hygiene), child marriage rates, child labor
- **How to cite:** "UNICEF, [Report Name], Bangladesh, [Year]"

### World Health Organization (WHO)
- **Bangladesh data:** https://www.who.int/countries/bgd
- **Global Health Observatory:** https://www.who.int/data/gho
- **What it covers:** Life expectancy, disease burden, health system data, vaccination coverage, maternal and child health
- **How to cite:** "WHO Global Health Observatory, [Indicator], [Year]"

### ILO â€” International Labour Organization
- **ILOSTAT Bangladesh:** https://ilostat.ilo.org/data/
- **What it covers:** Labor force participation rate, unemployment, youth employment, wage data, informality
- **How to cite:** "ILO ILOSTAT, [Indicator], [Year]"

## 5. SOURCE CONFLICT RESOLUTION

When two sources give different numbers for the same metric, follow this protocol:

### Step 1: Check if they're measuring the same thing
Different sources sometimes measure different things with the same label.
Example: "poverty rate" â€” BBS uses its own methodology; World Bank uses $2.15/day line.
If measuring different things: **use both, explain the difference**.

### Step 2: Check the time period
One source may be using a different reference year.
If time periods differ: **cite both with their respective years**.

### Step 3: Apply tier priority
If genuinely the same metric and same year:
1. Prefer Tier 1 (BBS, Bangladesh Bank) for Bangladesh-specific data
2. Prefer World Bank / IMF for GDP, macroeconomic indicators (their methodology is more consistent for international comparison)
3. Prefer UNESCO for education metrics

## 6. DEAD LINKS & DATA ACCESS ISSUES

Government portal websites (especially BBS) are sometimes slow, down, or reorganized.
Follow this fallback sequence:

1. Try the primary URL first
2. Try the Google Cache or Wayback Machine (web.archive.org) for the same URL
3. Try the World Bank version of the same data (they often republish BBS data)
4. If PDF is needed, try searching: `site:bbs.gov.bd [publication name] filetype:pdf`
5. If all fail: mark data as "source temporarily unavailable" and flag for human review

## 8. SOURCES WE DO NOT USE

| Source Type | Why Not |
|---|---|
| Anonymous social media posts | Unverifiable |
| Political party websites / press releases | Partisan, unverified |
| Wikipedia (as primary source) | Can be edited; use Wikipedia's cited sources instead |
| Personal blogs | No institutional accountability |
| Paywalled reports we cannot access | Cannot verify full methodology |
| Any source that itself cites "anonymous sources" for statistics | Circular, unverifiable |
