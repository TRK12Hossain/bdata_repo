## Research Summary
This research compiles inflation data for Bangladesh and its South Asian neighbors (India, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Nepal) for the period 2022-2024. The primary sources are the World Bank and the International Monetary Fund (IMF), which provide consistent annual average data suitable for cross-country comparison. Bangladesh Bank data, sourced from BBS, provides more recent monthly figures for Bangladesh specifically. Data for 2022 and 2023 is firm, while 2024 figures are largely projections from the IMF's World Economic Outlook. A key finding is that while all countries experienced inflationary pressure, the severity and trajectory varied significantly, with Sri Lanka and Pakistan facing exceptionally high rates.

## Source Conflicts & Discrepancies
- **Annual Average vs. Point-to-Point:** A key potential point of confusion is the difference between annual average inflation (used by IMF/World Bank for yearly comparisons) and point-to-point inflation (reported monthly by Bangladesh Bank/BBS). The annual average smooths out monthly volatility. For this report, annual averages are used for cross-country comparison, while the BB data is included for recent domestic context. No direct conflict was found, but the methodologies are different and should be noted.
- **Minor Statista vs. IMF/WB differences:** Some sources like Statista show slightly different figures (e.g., 9.02% for Bangladesh in 2023 vs. World Bank's 9.48%). This is likely due to different data update cycles or minor methodological adjustments. The IMF/World Bank figures are preferred as they are the primary source for international macroeconomic data and ensure consistency across countries.
